Implementing Guidelines for Practicum in the College of Information and Computing Sciences (CICS)

Introduction

InternConnect is a dedicated platform for requesting endorsement letters and viewing accredited companies in UST-CICS. It is part of the major requirements in the Practicum course for CICS prior to starting one's internship.

The curriculum of the College of Information and Computing Sciences includes practicum in all its three programs, namely:

  • CS 26116 - Computer Science
  • IS 26319 - Information Systems
  • IT 26212 - Information Technology

List of Practicum Units and Eligibility

Students are expected to pass the pre-requisite subjects for Practicum. Following that, CICS interns must complete the following required internship hours depending on their program and specialization track.

Computer Science - 250 hours

  • Specialization tracks: Core Computer Science, Game Development, Data Science
  • Units: 4
  • Pre-requisites: CS 26112, ICS 26013

Information Systems - 600 hours

  • Specialization tracks: Business Analytics, Service Management
  • Units: 6
  • Pre-requisites: IS 26316

Information Technology - 500 hours

  • Specialization tracks: Network and Security, Web and Mobile App Development, IT Automation
  • Units: 6
  • Pre-requisites: IT 26211

Only students who are officially enrolled for Practicum may undergo the internship program.

Issuance of Endorsement Letter

For the students to proceed with their requirements, it is necessary to fill out an endorsement form that can be accomplished through InternConnect. The endorsement form indicates the pertinent details about the company where a student intends to have the internship during the term. An endorsement letter will be generated based on the entries approved by the administrators on the form. it will explicitly state the lines of work that an intern must work on during the internship.

In accordance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012, the personal data of the users will be collected, recorded, and used only for academic purposes. To be specific, the personal data will be used to process the endorsement requests of the students currently enrolled in their practicum course in the College of Information and Computing Sciences (CICS).

The following information is gathered from students for the endorsement request in CICS:

  • Name
  • Student Email address
  • UST ID number
  • Department
  • Specialization track
  • Section
  • Contact information about the contact person from the Company
  • Acceptance letter from company

For selected administrators, the following information is collected to access the requests:

  • Admin Email address
  • E-signature
